Forests are an essential part of our ecosystem. They are a part of our everyday life and we don't even realise it. For example they are used in paper and wood products. The benefits of forests are vast. They provide us with clean air and water, prevent certain natural disasters, e.g. landslides, and are a crucial part of the ecosystems. (2) However there are many threats to a forest both natural and man-made. There are a number of human driven issues such as deforestation, camping, bonfires, logging and pollution. The natural threats to a forest are much more extreme than what a human can cause, these include wildfires, droughts, landslides, floods and even wildlife. Natural forest fires are ignited by lightning strikes (most-common), volcanic eruptions, rock falls(friction from falling rocks causing sparks) and even lightning strikes. Human factors can spur a forest fire just as much as natural processes, for example a cigarette being dumped.One of the main deliberate human factors that can exacerbate forest fires is logging. Wildfires are wild, uncontrollable and potentially destructive fires that affect both rural and urban areas. (1) I want to research a number of factors that affect a wildfire risk, including soil moisture and temperature. If the temperature is too high there is more of a risk of a wildfire starting. Conditions that cause fire favour to spread are usually 30 - 45°C, if there are dry conditions and very low humidity. Dry conditions are necessary for a fire to ignite therefore the dryer the soil the higher the risk of the forest becoming ignited. Wind plays a huge factor in the spread of wildfires as the stronger the wind the more the fire is going to travel.
